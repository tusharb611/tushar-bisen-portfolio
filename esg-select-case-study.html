Overview

A premium product with no way to sell itself

FactSet's ESG Select subscription unlocks deeper sustainability datasets sourced through third-party licensing agreements — including EMMI and other specialised providers. These datasets can't be included in the standard subscription due to licensing constraints.

The problem: users who would genuinely benefit from ESG Select had no visibility into what they were missing. There was no existing framework within FactSet for surfacing upsell moments inside the platform.

The challenge wasn't just driving upgrades — it was doing so without triggering the frustration of feeling nickel-and-dimed. These users had already invested significantly in their FactSet subscriptions. The approach had to feel like a natural extension of their journey, not a sales interruption.

Research & Discovery

Understanding the landscape

📊

Analytics-driven discovery

Platform analytics revealed specific touchpoints where users expected deeper ESG data but hit locked barriers — without understanding why. These became our prime upsell moments.

🤝

Stakeholder alignment

Worked with PMs and Sales to map common user objections, understand the business context, and identify which features users most frequently asked about post-lock.

🔍

Competitive benchmarking

Studied how other complex financial platforms handled gated content — identifying patterns that built user confidence versus those that drove churn or resentment.

🎯

Timing is everything

The key insight: the upsell had to surface at the moment of intent — when users were already seeking the exact data ESG Select provides — not as a proactive interruption.

"I didn't even know there was more data available. I just thought FactSet didn't have it."

— ESG Analyst, FactSet platform user (stakeholder research)

Problem Framing

The design challenge

"How might we surface ESG Select as a natural, value-driven extension of the user's existing journey — at exactly the right moment — without disrupting their task flow or creating feelings of pressure?"

This required creating FactSet's first in-platform upsell framework — one that balanced education, transparency, and trust while aligning with the expectations of a sophisticated financial user base.

Design Process

How we built the framework

01

Identify Intent Moments

Used analytics and stakeholder input to map specific touchpoints in the ESG Hub where users naturally looked for deeper data — these became anchor points for surfacing the upsell contextually.

02

Define the Communication Strategy

Crafted messaging that led with user benefit rather than cost. The tone had to be informative, not sales-y — explaining what ESG Select enables, not just that it exists.

03

Design the Teaser Component

Built a subtle embedded teaser within existing ESG workflows — dismissible, non-blocking, and designed to educate rather than interrupt. Explored placement in context with the locked data it unlocked.

04

Build Trust through Sample Access

Added access to a sample ESG Select report, allowing users to evaluate the actual depth of data before committing — a trust-building mechanism that reduced upgrade anxiety.

05

Iterate with Sales & Product

Ran design reviews across Sales, Product, and Engineering to align on messaging accuracy, conversion expectations, and technical constraints before shipping.

Reflection

Key learnings

What worked well

  • Leading with user benefit — not cost — made the upsell feel like a recommendation, not a sales pitch
  • Sample report access was the trust-building mechanism that made the difference for hesitant users
  • Surfacing at intent moments (when users hit a data gap) meant the message was always relevant
  • Involving Sales early ensured messaging aligned with what users actually asked about

What I'd do differently

  • A/B test different placements and copy variants to find the highest-converting combination
  • Design a clearer path for users who express interest but aren't decision-makers — connect them to Sales more explicitly
  • Track longer-term retention of upgraded users to validate that the value proposition matched reality
