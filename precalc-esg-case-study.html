Overview

The problem with waiting

Within FactSet's ESG Hub, data tables for portfolios and benchmarks require significant backend processing before results can be displayed. These calculations involve fetching, validating, and transforming data from multiple sources — often taking several minutes.

Users had zero visibility into this process. They were stuck watching an indefinite spinner, unable to navigate away without restarting the entire calculation from scratch. In a platform where analysts manage multiple portfolios simultaneously, this was a critical productivity bottleneck.

If a user navigated away mid-calculation, the entire process restarted — wasting minutes of compute time and creating deep frustration for power users who needed to work across multiple portfolios in a single session.

Research

What users were telling us

As the sole designer, I led research through user interviews, session observations, and direct feedback from ESG Hub power users to understand the depth and frequency of the problem.

⏳

Forced idle time

Users sat unproductively while data loaded — unable to work on other tables or portfolios without losing their calculation progress.

🔄

Restart loops

Any accidental navigation reset the calculation entirely. Users who managed multiple portfolios hit this repeatedly in a single session.

👻

No system feedback

The indefinite spinner gave no indication of progress, estimated time, or whether the calculation had stalled or was still running.

📊

Power user demand

ESG Hub power users explicitly asked for parallel workflows — the ability to trigger multiple calculations and monitor their progress simultaneously.

"I can't do anything while it loads. I'll switch to another tab and come back to find it reset. I've started just leaving it running overnight."

— ESG Hub Power User (research interview)

Problem Framing

Design challenges

"How might we let users initiate data calculations without being blocked — while giving them full visibility into progress so they can multitask with confidence?"

Reduce opaque loading

Replace the indefinite spinner with meaningful, real-time progress visibility across all active calculations.

Enable async workflows

Let users trigger pre-calculations in the background and navigate freely without losing their work.

Surface system state

Make the system's calculation status legible at a glance — what's calculating, what's ready, what needs attention.

Design Process

How we approached it

01

Research & Problem Definition

Conducted user interviews and session observations with ESG power users. Mapped the current-state calculation workflow and identified all failure points, user workarounds, and unmet expectations.

02

Concept Exploration

Explored multiple approaches — including inline progress states, a global notification bar, and a dedicated pre-calculation panel. Evaluated each against the core goals of discoverability, control, and minimal workflow disruption.

03

Defining the 4 States

Designed a clear state machine for each table: Not Loaded → Data Expired → Calculating → Calculated. Each state communicates system status and surfaces the right action at the right time.

04

Stakeholder Validation & Iteration

Ran design reviews with Product, Engineering, and senior ESG users. Refined the panel layout, state transitions, and action hierarchy across 3 major iterations.

05

Engineering Handoff

Delivered annotated Figma specs including all states, edge cases, and loading patterns. Stayed closely involved during build to ensure fidelity and address implementation questions in real time.

Outcomes

Impact

⏱

↓10 min idle time per session

Users no longer needed to sit blocked while data loaded. Pre-calculation freed them to work on other portfolios or analysis tasks in parallel.

⚡

+30% workflow efficiency

Enabling asynchronous data preparation across multiple portfolios dramatically improved how much users could accomplish in a single session.

🎛

Improved user control

Progress tracking and non-blocking UX gave users a sense of agency over the system — a key shift from frustration to confidence.

📈

Boosted perceived reliability

Visibility into system activity significantly improved users' perception of ESG Hub's speed and reliability, even when actual compute time didn't change.

Reflection

Key learnings

What worked well

  • Designing the full state machine (4 states) first ensured every transition felt intentional and complete
  • Power user interviews early on grounded the solution in real frustration — not assumed pain
  • Keeping the feature as a panel (not a separate page) minimised context-switching
  • Close engineering collaboration prevented late-stage scope changes

What I'd do differently

  • Establish baseline metrics (avg idle time, restart frequency) before launch to better quantify the improvement
  • Design a notification system so users know their calculations are ready without returning to check
  • Explore onboarding for new users who may not discover the pre-calculate feature organically
